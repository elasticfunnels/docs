# Fulfillment Overview
---

Overview of automated order fulfillment with third‑party providers in ElasticFunnels.

## What it does (in short)
- Sends your paid orders to a fulfillment provider (like ShipOffers) after a short, configurable delay
- Lets you track status (pending, sent, failed, cancelled) and shipment updates
- Lets you cancel or re‑send when needed

## Quick start
1) Add a fulfillment integration
- Go to Integrations → Fulfillment → choose a provider (e.g., ShipOffers Order Management)
- Click “Add”, enter the required credentials, and Save

2) Link your products to that provider
- Go to Products → Edit → Fulfillment & Warehousing
- Set “Fulfillment Provider” (e.g., ShipOffers)
- Pick “Fulfillment Integration” (your saved integration instance)
- Save the product

3) Let orders flow
- New paid orders are scheduled automatically and then sent to the provider after the delay window
- Check status from the Fulfillment screen in Analytics/Orders

## Before you start
- Make sure your product SKUs match what your warehouse expects
- Have valid shipping details for customers
- Obtain provider credentials (API keys, store IDs, etc.)
- Decide on a default shipping service (Standard/Express) if your provider uses it
 - Decide your initial delay before sending orders (measured in hours in the UI)

## Where to manage and monitor
- Integrations → Fulfillment: add/manage provider connections
- Products → Fulfillment & Warehousing: link each product to a provider + integration
- Orders/Analytics → Fulfillment: see queue/status, cancel or resend if necessary
